16 Barangays

When Aklan separated from Capiz on April 25, 1956, New Washington became one of the seventeen (17) municipalities of the newly created province. Presently, New Washington has comprised of sixteen (16) barangays; eight (8) barangays in the coastal areas, and eight (8) in the farmlands.

SANDUGUAN SHRINE

Ochando, New Washington, Aklan

A historical marker constructed in honor of the eight (8) signatories of blood compact who expressed defiance against Spanish tyrants. It symbolically represents the cultural heritage of the people of New Washington as its historic contribution to the Katipunan - the First Philippine Revolutionary Movement.

CARDINAL SIN MONUMENT

Poblacion, New Washington, Aklan

A statue in honor of Jaime Cardinal Sin, a native of New Washington. He was instrumental during the “People Power Revolution”, a peaceful revolution against Pres. Ferdinand Marcos.



TALABAHAN

Tambak, New Washington, Aklan

A sprawling seafood resort establishment found along Cardinal Sin Avenue in Barangay Tambak, fronting the concrete seawall overviewing the Sibuyan Sea. At sundown, the seafood village is a wholesome treat for the entire family.
